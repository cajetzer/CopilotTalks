## Purpose

The Spine is a 7-slide shared context-setter that runs before any of the three standalone executive briefings. It establishes the vocabulary and framing every executive audience needs — regardless of which talk they're receiving — so each talk can open directly into its unique value without spending the first 15 minutes re-establishing context.

## The 7 Slides

### 1. Why This Briefing Is Different
Not a technology evaluation. An investment decision about organizational capacity and competitive position.

### 2. The 67% Problem
McKinsey 2023: developers spend only 30–33% of time writing new code. AI coding tools — the category most organizations have invested in — address only the minority of software delivery labor. The 67% is the structured information processing that surrounds code: triage, planning, compliance review, documentation, validation, knowledge transfer.

### 3. The Shift: From Coders to Captains
The operating model reframe. A commercial pilot doesn't hand-fly for most of the journey — autopilot handles mechanical flight. The pilot plans missions, makes go/no-go decisions, monitors instruments, and takes responsibility for every landing. Agentic AI is the autopilot. Developers are becoming pilots.

### 4. Why Instruments Are Non-Optional
Autopilot without instruments is dangerous, not productive. One pilot can supervise 3–4 concurrent agentic sessions simultaneously — but only with automated test health, security posture, compliance status, and deployment-window indicators. Without the instrument panel, adding agents adds risk, not capacity.

### 5. The Flight System
The organizational reframe: it's not just pilots and planes. FAA (Security/Compliance/Legal) → ATC (CTO/VP Eng) → Ground Crew (Platform/DevOps) → Captains (Developers) → Autopilot (Agents). Every role is essential. Most organizations have the Pilots and the Autopilot but are missing Ground Crew and instruments.

### 6. The Labor Arbitrage
Agents execute at $2–5/hour (API cost). Human engineers bill at $70–150/hour loaded cost. The arbitrage is real — but only when the infrastructure overhead of supervising agents is automated. Manual review of agent output eliminates the cost advantage.

### 7. The Competitive Window
DORA 2023: elite software delivery teams deploy 208× more frequently than low performers. These teams started investing in the infrastructure 18–24 months ago. The gap compounds. Organizations building the instrument panel in 2025–2026 are establishing an advantage that cannot be compressed into a shorter timeline by a later entrant.
